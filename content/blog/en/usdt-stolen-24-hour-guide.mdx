## Introduction: Why Are the First 24 Hours So Critical?

The moment you discover your USDT or other cryptocurrency has been stolen, your heart may skip a beat. But remember: **panic is the enemy of recovery**.

Based on data from 357+ cases handled by the USDTRecovery team over the past three years, we have identified the following key patterns:

- **Action within 0-2 hours**: Recovery success rate of approximately **35-40%**
- **Action within 2-6 hours**: Recovery success rate of approximately **25-30%**
- **Action within 6-24 hours**: Recovery success rate of approximately **15-20%**
- **After 7 days**: Recovery success rate drops sharply to **below 5%**

Why is time so important? Because:

1. **Hackers are moving funds**: Most thieves immediately distribute funds to multiple wallets after the theft
2. **Exchanges can freeze**: If you notify exchanges before funds enter them, there's a chance to freeze accounts
3. **Evidence disappears**: Although the blockchain is transparent, tracking difficulty increases exponentially over time

This article will walk you through the golden 24 hours after cryptocurrency theft. Every step is based on real case studies—please read carefully and act immediately.

## First Phase (0-2 Hours): Emergency Damage Control

The first 2 hours after discovering the theft are your last chance to **stop further losses**. The core goal of this phase is: **secure remaining assets and preserve existing evidence**.

### ✅ Action Checklist

#### 1. Immediately Check Other Assets

Hackers usually don't target just one wallet. You need to:

- **Check all addresses under the same seed phrase**: Including ETH, BSC, Polygon, and all other EVM chains
- **Check other wallets**: If you have multiple wallets, verify balances one by one
- **Check funds in DeFi protocols**: Liquidity in Uniswap, Aave, and other protocols may also be stolen
- **Check NFT holdings**: View OpenSea, Blur, and other platforms for abnormal transfers

**⚠️ Note**: Don't rely solely on mobile app displays—always check real-time balances on blockchain explorers (Etherscan, BscScan, etc.).

#### 2. Disconnect Suspicious Connections

If you're using a hot wallet (MetaMask, Trust Wallet, etc.), immediately perform the following actions:

| Action | Specific Steps | Priority |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| Revoke authorizations | Use Revoke.cash or Unrekt.net to check and revoke all suspicious authorizations | 🔴 Highest |
| Disconnect website connections | Disconnect all DApp connections in wallet settings | 🔴 Highest |
| Remove suspicious plugins | Check browser plugins and remove recently installed suspicious extensions | 🟡 High |
| Switch devices | If you suspect device compromise, immediately switch to a clean device | 🟡 High |

**Detailed steps for revoking authorizations**:

1. Visit https://revoke.cash
2. Connect your wallet (read-only mode is safer)
3. View all authorized contracts
4. Click "Revoke" for any suspicious or unknown authorizations
5. Confirm the transaction in your wallet (small gas fees will apply)

#### 3. Save Critical Evidence

This is the most easily overlooked but **crucial** step. You need to immediately screenshot and save:

**Evidence you must preserve:**

- [ ] Transaction hash (Tx Hash) of the stolen transaction
- [ ] Stolen amount and time
- [ ] Attacker wallet address
- [ ] Your wallet address
- [ ] Authorization records (if malicious authorization is involved)
- [ ] Any emails or messages related to the attack

**Storage format**:

```
Recommended format:
📁 Theft Evidence/
  ├── 📄 Transaction Record Screenshots/
  │   ├── tx_0x123..._main.png
  │   └── tx_0x456..._internal.png
  ├── 📄 Wallet Info.txt
  ├── 📄 Timeline.md
  └── 📄 Related Chat History/
```

<Alert type="info">
  **Pro Tip**: Use blockchain explorers (like Etherscan) to export complete transaction records as CSV—this will be your core evidence for reporting to police and recovery efforts.
</Alert>

## Second Phase (2-6 Hours): On-Chain Tracking

After stopping the damage, you need to **understand the enemy's movements**. On-chain tracking is the foundation for determining recovery strategy.

### ✅ Action Checklist

#### 1. Use Blockchain Explorers for Deep Analysis

Select the appropriate explorer based on the stolen token:

| Token | Mainnet | Recommended Explorer |
|-------|---------|---------------------|
| USDT/USDC | Ethereum | Etherscan.io |
| USDT/USDC | BSC (BEP20) | BscScan.com |
| USDT/USDC | TRON (TRC20) | Tronscan.org |
| USDT/USDC | Polygon | Polygonscan.com |
| USDT/USDC | Arbitrum | Arbiscan.io |
| USDT/USDC | Optimism | Optimistic.etherscan.io |

**Tracking steps:**

1. **View transaction details**: Enter Tx Hash to see complete transaction information
2. **Track fund flows**: Click "From" and "To" addresses to view other attacker activities
3. **Identify exchange addresses**: Compare with known exchange deposit addresses (available on exchange websites)
4. **Flag suspicious addresses**: Use the browser's "Watch List" feature for ongoing monitoring

#### 2. Record All Transaction Hashes

Every stolen fund transaction needs to be recorded:

```
Transaction Record Template:

Stolen Transaction #1
- Tx Hash: 0x...
- Time: 2026-03-03 14:32:15 (UTC+8)
- Amount: 50,000 USDT
- Attacker address: 0x...
- Current status: Transferred to Binance hot wallet

Stolen Transaction #2
- Tx Hash: 0x...
- ...
```

#### 3. Identify Fund Destination

Based on our case database, common destination patterns for stolen funds:

**Pattern A: Direct transfer to exchange (highest recovery rate)**
- Funds enter centralized exchanges like Binance, OKX, Huobi
- If exchanges are notified promptly, freezing success rate is about 60-70%

**Pattern B: Through mixers (medium recovery rate)**
- Using mixing protocols like Tornado Cash
- Requires professional on-chain analysis tools for tracking

**Pattern C: Cross-chain transfer (low recovery rate)**
- Transferred to other chains via cross-chain bridges
- Tracking is difficult but not impossible

**Pattern D: DeFi laundering (lowest recovery rate)**
- Exchanged for other tokens through DEXs
- Then dispersed to multiple addresses

<Alert type="success">
  **Good News**: In the 357+ cases we've handled, **approximately 42% of funds eventually enter centralized exchanges**. As long as tracking is timely and exchanges are contacted, there's a good chance of freezing.
</Alert>

## Third Phase (6-24 Hours): Seek Professional Help

Individual efforts are limited—**leave professional matters to professionals**.

### ✅ Action Checklist

#### 1. Contact Professional Agencies

At this stage, you need to assess whether professional help is needed:

**Scenarios for handling yourself:**
- Smaller amounts (< $5,000)
- Funds clearly entered a well-known exchange
- You have time and ability to follow up continuously

**Scenarios requiring professional agencies:**
- Larger amounts (> $10,000)
- Complex fund destinations involving mixing or multi-chain transfers
- Requires judicial resolution
- Time-sensitive, needs rapid response

**Criteria for selecting professional agencies:**

| Evaluation Dimension | Qualified Standard |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Success cases | Real, verifiable successful recovery cases |
| Transparent pricing | Clear service fees, no hidden charges |
| Compliance | Proper company registration and legal qualifications |
| Technical capability | Professional on-chain analysis capabilities |
| Confidentiality agreement | Strict confidentiality agreement signed |

#### 2. Prepare Police Report Materials

If you decide to report to police, prepare the following materials:

**Basic materials:**
- [ ] Identity documents
- [ ] Police report (explaining the theft process)
- [ ] Wallet ownership proof
- [ ] Complete transaction records

**Technical evidence:**
- [ ] Blockchain explorer screenshots
- [ ] Transaction hash list
- [ ] Attacker address analysis report
- [ ] Fund flow diagram

**Value proof:**
- [ ] Token price proof at time of theft (CoinMarketCap screenshot)
- [ ] Fund source proof (purchase records, withdrawal records)

<Alert type="info">
  **Police Report Location Selection**:
  - Priority: Cybersecurity department of public security bureau in your location
  - If you know the attacker is domestic, you can also choose police in the attacker's location
  - For cases involving huge amounts (>500,000 RMB), you can directly contact provincial cybersecurity corps

#### 3. Initiate Judicial Proceedings

For cases with larger amounts, judicial proceedings are the most effective approach:

**Civil approach:**
- Apply to court for property preservation
- Sue the exchange receiving stolen funds (if known)
- Apply for investigation orders to obtain exchange information

**Criminal approach:**
- Report to public security organs, request case filing
- Cooperate with police for technical appraisal
- Coordinate through Interpol (if funds flow overseas)

**Judicial cooperation:**
- Work with professional law firms
- Cooperate with on-chain investigation companies for technical reports
- Contact overseas exchanges through compliance channels

## After 24 Hours: Continuous Follow-up

After the golden 24 hours, the case enters the **long-term battle phase**. Patience and persistence are key.

### ✅ Action Checklist

#### 1. Case Progress Tracking

Establish a regular follow-up mechanism:

- **Weekly**: Check if attacker addresses have new activity
- **Monthly**: Update police report progress, supplement new evidence
- **Quarterly**: Assess whether strategy adjustments are needed

Use tools for continuous monitoring:
- Etherscan's Watch List
- Arkham Intelligence
- Chainalysis Reactor (used by professional agencies)

#### 2. Maintain Communication with Exchanges

If funds entered an exchange:

1. **Regularly inquire about progress**: Politely but firmly follow up on freezing status
2. **Provide supplementary materials**: Submit new evidence promptly if available
3. **Understand judicial cooperation process**: Ask what legal documents are needed for cooperation
4. **Document communication history**: Keep all emails and chat records

#### 3. Legal Assistance

Depending on case progress, you may need:

- **Hire professional lawyers**: Especially when involving overseas exchanges
- **Apply for judicial appraisal**: Technical appraisal of on-chain evidence
- **International judicial assistance**: Request overseas investigations through MLAT treaties

## Common Myths FAQ

### ❌ Myth #1: "Just Wait for the Police to Handle It"

**Truth**: Most police departments lack experience with cryptocurrency cases, with filing rates below 30%. You need to actively push forward, and may even need to provide technical analysis reports.

**Correct approach**: Continuously follow up after reporting, proactively provide technical support, and hire professional lawyers to push for filing if necessary.

### ❌ Myth #2: "Contacting the Exchange Yourself Will Solve It"

**Truth**: Individual contact with exchanges often doesn't get effective response. Exchanges receive numerous similar requests daily—only formal letters with legal effect or judicial assistance requests are prioritized.

**Correct approach**: Send formal letters through professional agencies or lawyers, or wait for police to issue investigation letters.

### ❌ Myth #3: "Believing 'Guaranteed Recovery' Promises"

**Truth**: Any agency promising "100% recovery" or "no success no fee but pay deposit first" should be treated with caution. Blockchain tracking is technical work—no one can guarantee results.

**Correct approach**: Choose agencies with transparent pricing and real cases, sign formal contracts, and avoid prepaying large amounts.

### ❌ Myth #4: "Small Amounts Aren't Worth Pursuing"

**Truth**: Many small cases actually come from the same attack gang, with cumulative amounts being substantial. Timely reporting helps police conduct joint case investigations.

**Correct approach**: Regardless of amount, preserve evidence and report to police.
