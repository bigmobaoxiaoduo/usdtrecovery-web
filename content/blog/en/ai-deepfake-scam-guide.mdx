## Chapter 1: How AI Deepfake Technology Works (Simplified)

### 1.1 What is Deepfake?

Deepfake uses deep learning to generate fake audio and video content. It mainly includes two forms:

**Face Swapping**
- Maps one person's facial features onto another's video
- Requires photos/videos for training (usually 20 minutes of material is sufficient)
- Real-time versions can achieve video call-level forgery

**Voice Cloning**
- Clones anyone's voice with just 3-10 seconds of audio sample
- Can generate speech content for any text
- Supports real-time voice conversion (changing voice during calls)

### 1.2 How Do Scammers Get Training Material?

You might wonder: how do scammers get my videos and voice?

**Common Channels:**

| Channel | Method | Risk Level |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Social Media | Your posted TikToks, Instagram Reels | 🔴 Extremely High |
| Video Conferences | Hacking meeting software or recording public meetings | 🟠 High |
| Customer Service Calls | Posing as customer service to get you talking | 🟠 High |
| Black Market Purchases | Buying personal info from data breaches | 🔴 Extremely High |
| Phishing Calls | Proactively calling to collect voice samples | 🟡 Medium |

**Real Case**:
> In January 2025, a user had posted over 30 personal videos on TikTok, which scammers used to train their AI model. The scammers forged a video call to his parents asking for money, successfully stealing 180,000 USDT.

### 1.3 The Technical Barrier for Real-Time Deepfakes

You might think this technology is complex and only professional hackers can operate it. But the reality is:

## Chapter 2: Common AI Scam Tactics

### 2.1 Tactic 1: "I'm Your Son/Daughter"

**Target**: Middle-aged and elderly crypto investors

**Method**:
1. Obtain family photos and children's videos from social media
2. Use AI to clone the child's voice and partial facial features
3. Call parents: "Mom/Dad, I lost my phone, using a friend's phone"
4. Fabricate an emergency (kidnapped, car accident, arrested)
5. Demand immediate USDT transfer to a "safe account"

**Why it works**:
- Exploits parents' worry for their children
- Voice cloning technology can already be indistinguishable from real
- Emergencies leave no time for verification

### 2.2 Tactic 2: Fake Customer Service Video Verification

**Target**: Exchange users

**Method**:
1. Send phishing SMS: "Your account has suspicious activity and needs video verification"
2. Guide users to download a "customer service app" (actually malware)
3. "Customer service" shows forged employee ID and office environment via video call
4. Request mnemonic phrases or private keys for "identity verification"
5. Or induce users to make a "test transfer"

**Variants**:
- Posing as Binance, OKX customer service
- Posing as wallet app support (MetaMask, Trust Wallet)
- Posing as DeFi protocol technical support

### 2.3 Tactic 3: Pig Butchering 2.0

**Target**: Single people seeking emotional connection

**Typical Process**:
1. Match with "ideal partner" on social/dating apps
2. They initiate video calls (AI-forged) to build trust
3. Cultivate relationship for 1-2 weeks
4. Introduce "investment opportunity," showing "their" profit screenshots

**Target**: Company employees, finance personnel

## Chapter 3: How to Identify AI Deepfakes

### 3.1 Video Forgery Detection Techniques

**Observe Facial Details:**

| Check Item | Normal Video | AI-Forged Video |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Blinking Frequency | Natural, ~15-20 times/min | May be too fast, slow, or no blinking |
| Lip Sync | Perfectly matches audio | May have slight misalignment |
| Facial Edges | Clear and natural | May be blurry or jittery |
| Eye Reflection | Consistent direction in both eyes | May be inconsistent or missing |
| Skin Texture | Pores, fine lines visible | May be too smooth or unnatural |

**Test Methods:**

**1. Profile Test**
- Ask the person to show their side profile
- Most deepfakes reveal flaws at side angles

**2. Occlusion Test**
- Cover part of the face with your hand
- AI face swaps usually deform in occluded areas

**3. Rapid Movement Test**
- Ask them to turn their head quickly or shake
- Deepfakes tend to "lose face" during rapid movement

**4. Lighting Change Test**
- Change ambient lighting (turn lights on/off)
- Forged faces may not respond correctly to light changes

### 3.2 Voice Cloning Detection Techniques

**Audio Clues:**

- **Abnormal background noise**: Cloned voices may have strange electronic artifacts
- **Monotonous intonation**: AI speech may lack emotional variation
- **Missing breath sounds**: Real people breathe while speaking; AI may not
- **Specific pronunciation**: Complex words may sound unnatural

**Test Methods:**

**1. Interference Test**
- Create background noise (play music)
- Ask them to repeat complex sentences in the noise
- AI tends to make mistakes in noisy environments

**2. Impromptu Questioning**
- Ask questions only the real person would know
- "What's the name of the restaurant where we last ate together?"
- AI cannot answer untrained information

**3. Dialect/Language Test**
- Switch to a dialect or language they're unfamiliar with
- AI usually can only clone the language used in training

## Chapter 4: Prevention Strategies and Emergency Measures

### 4.1 Prevention-First Protection Strategy

**Social Media Privacy Settings:**

```
✅ Must Do:
□ Set social media accounts to private
□ Turn off "allow strangers to view my videos"
□ Delete or hide old videos with clear face and voice
□ Turn off platform "AI training" data authorization (like Meta's AI data usage)

⚠️ Special Attention:
□ Don't publicly share passport/ID photos
□ Don't read sensitive information in videos
□ Don't participate in "voice challenges" that may collect voiceprints
```

**Establish Identity Verification Code Words:**

Agree with family and important contacts:
- **Emergency code word**: A specific word only you both know
- **Reverse verification**: After they say the code word, you must say one too
- **Secondary confirmation**: For large transfers, confirm through another method (in person, known backup phone)

### 4.2 Pre-Transfer Checklist

**No matter who the recipient is, complete this before transferring:**

| Check Item | Method | Time |
|-----------|--------|------|
| Multi-channel Confirmation | Contact to confirm via another known channel | 2 minutes |
| Set Test Question | Ask something only the real person would know | 1 minute |
| Small Amount Test | First transfer 1 USDT, confirm receipt before larger amount | 5 minutes |
| Delay Transfer | If they demand "immediate" transfer, that's a red flag | - |
| Cooling-off Period | Any "emergency" should have time for verification | 10-30 minutes |

### 4.3 Emergency Response If Scammed

**If you suspect you've encountered an AI scam:**

**Immediate Actions:**

1. **Stop the transfer** - No matter how urgent they are
2. **Multi-channel verification** - Immediately contact the person through other means
3. **Screenshot/record evidence** - Including chat history, transfer records
4. **Contact the exchange** - If funds have entered an exchange, immediately request a freeze
5. **Report to police** - Provide AI scam evidence; police need to understand this new type of crime

**Evidence Collection Priority:**

- Complete video call recording (if available)
- Phone numbers/accounts used by the scammer
- Transfer transaction hash (Tx Hash)
- All chat records
- Your original judgment basis (why you believed them)
